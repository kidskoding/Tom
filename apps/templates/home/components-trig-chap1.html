Chapter 1: Review - Geometry

A triangle is a type of two-dimensional shape that has three angles and three sides (that’s how it gets its name). The slanted side is what we call the hypotenuse and the other two sides are what we call the legs Its angles also add up to 180 degrees. 

Triangles can be classified into three main types

  1. Equilateral - A triangle in which every side and angle are equal
  2. Scalene - A triangle in which neither the sides nor angles are equal
  3. Isosceles - A triangle in which two sides and two angles are equal

In triangles, congruency is simply the idea that two triangles have equal size and shape. With triangles, there are multiple ways to prove triangle congruency

  • SSS - Side Side Side. Two triangles can be proved congruent if all three of their sides are equal
  • SAS - Side Angle Side. Two triangles can be proved congruent if two sides and the angle in between the two sides are congruent 
  • AAS - Angle Angle Side. Two triangles can be proved congruent if two angles and a side (non-included) are equal
  • ASA - Angle Side Angle. Two triangles can be proved congruent if two angles and a side in between are equal
  • HL - If the hypotenuse and leg of a right triangle equal the hypotenuse and leg of another right triangle, the two triangles can be proved congruent

A scale factor is a factor that when applied to a triangle changes the sides (area, and volume), but not the angles. A scale factor must be equally distributed among all three sides of a triangle. For example, if I multiply one side of a triangle by 1/2, I must also multiply the rest of the sides by 1/2.


A right triangle is a special type of triangle where one angle is 90 degrees. A right triangle is great because it allows for more enhancement of special features and formulas like the Pythagorean theorem and SOH CAH TOA

The Pythagorean Theorem states that for any given right triangle,

        a^2 + b^2 = c^2, where a and b are legs and c is the hypotenuse

If two sides of a triangle are known, the third side can be found using the Pythagorean theorem

SOH CAH TOA is an easy way to remember the three primary trigonometric functions. In a full-fledged trigonometry course, there is a huge extension to this, but in geometry, there are three main functions. The trigonometric functions express the ratios between the sides of a triangle (Even when multiplied by a scale factor, the sides are still going to have the same ratio)

The sine of an angle is referred to as the ratio between the angle's opposite side and its hypotenuse side. 

The cosine of an angle is also referred to as the ratio between the angle's adjacent side and its hypotenuse side. The prefix co means complementary so the cosine of an angle is actually just the sine of its complementary angle

The tangent of an angle is referred to as the ratio between the angle's opposite side and its adjacent side.
